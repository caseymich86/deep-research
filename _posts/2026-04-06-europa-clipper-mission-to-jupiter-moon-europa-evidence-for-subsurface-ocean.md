## The Hook

On March 5, 2019, NASA's Associate Administrator for the Science Mission Directorate, Thomas Zurbuchen, announced that the Interior Characterization of Europa using Magnetometry (ICEMAG) instrument would no longer be part of the mission due to recurring cost increases over three times the original cost of $100 million.

## The Debate
Researcher Carol Raymond proposed the Interior Characterization of Europa using Magnetometry (ICEMAG) instrument to determine Europa's ocean depth, salinity, and ice shell thickness on the Europa Clipper mission, which was initially scheduled for launch in 2022 but delayed due to budget constraints. Thomas Zurbuchen, NASA's Associate Administrator for the Science Mission Directorate, cancelled ICEMAG's inclusion in March 2019 due to cost increases over three times the original proposal of $150 million.

The cancellation of ICEMAG means that a less complex magnetometer will be included on the mission, which may limit the ability to detect erupting plume activity and determine the subsurface ocean's characteristics, such as its temperature and composition.
